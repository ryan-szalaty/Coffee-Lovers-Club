Welcome to Coffee Lover's Club

"Worry not about the coffee, but be wary of the coughee: he who is coughed upon." - Ryan Szalaty

Crisp autumn mornings complete with the brisk aroma of a dark roast coffee sitting idyllicly on your porch. Sweet, creamy clouds atop an ice-cold frappacino before your first college class of the day. Bold, yet swift burst of energy for those late nights you put in.

No matter where you are in the world, one thing brings us all together: coffee.

Perhaps you are brand new to coffee and would like to take your first step into this lovely world. Or, perhaps you have been an avid consumer of coffee for as long as you can remember. Anyone is welcome in the Coffee Lover's Club.

Have a minute? Pour yourself a cup and relax. Let's learn about some interesting coffee facts.

Fact #1: Coffee was feared long ago.

Why, you wonder, would anyone react with fear to something so wonderful? The wonder of coffee, in fact, was the source of the fear for those in power.

Coffee houses were popular areas where people gathered to discuss matters of all sorts. These matters were thought to be conspiracies.

So, the next time someone says that coffee is bad for you, remind them of the power it had to give powerful people a headache.

Fact #2: Coffee was discovered by a goat.

Trekking through the vast, open fields of Ethiopia, a goat came across a strange tree blooming with coffee cherries. Naturally, the curious goat decided to eat one.

One cherry became many, and with many coffee cherries, the goat's amazing energy levels caught the attention of humans.

Be sure to thank a goat next time you see one. Just don't give it any coffee.

Fact #3: College was the same price as a cup of coffee back in the day.

Centuries ago, for the price of a single coffee, you could walk into a coffeehouse and speak with some of the best minds in Europe.

It was like having unlimited office hours with your favorite professor for a much more affordable rate.

And the best part? No application, SATs or financial aid required.

Fact #4: Coffee helps you live longer.

Caffeine gets a bad rap for the benefits it can give you, namely that of increased energy and mental awareness.

More energy means being less prone to mistakes and accidents as a result of fatigue or too many late nights playing Skyrim.

As with all things, take it in moderation. Except Skyrim. It deserves to be played for an unhealthy amount of time.

Fact #5: Decaf is not completely caffeine-free.

There are a couple of ways to extract the caffeine from coffee beans while retaining the flavor compounds.

However, none of these methods can remove all traces of caffeine. About 3% always remains in your cup.

Remind someone of that fact they next time they look down on you through their "decaf".

What's the difference between mild, medium and dark roasts?

Coffee consumers have a certain preference for the kind of coffee they like, including purists who enjoy it without milk or sweeteners. Depending on bean roasting time and temperature, the flavor profile of your coffee will change.

Mild coffee, with the least intensive roasting, actually contains more caffeine than the other roasts. This is because the caffeine is not broken down as much due to lower temperatures and roasting times. Mild coffee is therefore more acidic and has a lighter body, making it quite pleasant to drink.

Medium coffee sits between mild and dark, having a delicate balance between the brightness of a mild roast and the strong, rich flavor of a dark roast coffee. Quite a few flavor blends are made from medium coffee due to the balance it offers, such as the all-time-favorite hazlenut. Other blends provide a chocolately or fruity undertone as well.

Dark coffee takes the cake for having the most intensive roasting process, and in turn, having carmelized sugars within the beans. This results in smoky or bitter undertone with a possible burnt taste. In addition, dark roast coffee usually has the least amount of caffeine because of the break down during roasting. A great choice for those who like robust flavors.
